# Business Insights: Netflix Content Strategy & Viewer Engagement

## Executive Findings

1. **Genre retention is uneven.** High-completion genres should receive stronger recommendation placement, renewal priority, and localized marketing support.
2. **Mobile users show binge-friendly behavior.** Mobile viewers create frequent short sessions and should receive episode-continuation nudges, offline-download prompts, and short-form recommendation rails.
3. **Premium users drive disproportionate revenue.** Premium adoption is a strategic lever; high-engagement Standard users are clear upgrade candidates.
4. **Churn rises when engagement drops.** Low watch minutes plus weak completion is the strongest early warning pattern for retention campaigns.
5. **Weekend watch time spikes.** Friday-Sunday release scheduling should be prioritized for tentpole content and genre-specific campaigns.

## Recommended Actions

- Build lifecycle campaigns around low-engagement users before they churn.
- Create country-specific genre rows for high-affinity local markets.
- Promote high-IMDB but low-discovery content through homepage experiments.
- Use binge-day detection to trigger next-episode and collection recommendations.
- Review content renewal decisions using engagement score, completion, reviews, and acquisition value together.
